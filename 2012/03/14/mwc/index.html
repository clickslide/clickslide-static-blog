Jatrobot & ClickSlide

Posted by Aaron Franco Posted on Mar - 14 - 2012

This year we participated in two hackathons at Mobile World Congress in Barcelona. The first was hosted by the U.N.’s ITU, Nokia, Microsoft, Telefonica & BlueVia, and the EU Commision. The goal was to create an application (web, mobile, or desktop) that helped promote “green” energy. Our initial idea to create a robot that plants trees didn’t quite fit with that goal so we had to adapt the idea on the spot. We did a bit of research and discovered the Jatropha Curcas. A plant that can be used to create bio diesel.

The hackathon was a two day event with a total of 8 hours available for “hacking”. We spent the first day researching the Jatropha plant and technology needed for the robot. The second day we built the robot and implemented the mobile application with ClickSlide. You can read more about our pitch here.

We ended up winning a Nokia Lumia 800 for Best Windows Phone Implementation! It was a proud day!

The second hackathon we participated in was during the WIP Jam session in the main auditoriums. It was hosted by Nokia as a way to promote their new Location API. For this event we decided to add the location based service to our Jatrobot app. Luckily for us, at the Nokia booth they were demoing their new Indoor Positioning API. We were able to get our hands on a data file for that system, and we integrated it into the robot. So, our final application was a mobile application that not only tracked the location of the user, but also tracked the position of the Jatropha Curcas plant on the farm.

We were awarded two Nokia Lumia 800 phones for first place, best Social / Location aware application using Nokia’s Location API! Great work to the team, we really pulled together for this one.

Come see us pitch at London Web Summit on March 19th.
